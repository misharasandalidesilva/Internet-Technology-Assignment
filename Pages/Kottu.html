Kottu Recipe

This recipe for kottu roti, a popular Sri Lankan street food, comes from Sanjeewa Gooneratne, who prepares the dish at events around New York on a griddle the size of a sled. A flaky flatbread is stir-fried with eggs and spices and finished with a curry sauce. Don't let the long list of spices deter you from making the dish. You can omit a few and still experience its fantastic depths of flavor.

  • Cheese Kottu

Chicken Kottu

Ingredients of Chicken Kottu

For the Kottu Base
  • 4-6 pieces of roti (paratha) or flatbread (store-bought or homemade)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
For the Stir-fry
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, mincedr
  • 1-inch piece ginger, minced
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 1 cup cabbage, shredded
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped (optional)
  • 1 cup cooked chicken, chopped into small pieces (see notes below)
  • 2-3 eggs, beaten (optional for extra protein)
For Seasoning
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • ½ teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon tomato sauce (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

Instructions

1. Prepare the Roti
  • Cook the Roti: If you’re using store-bought roti, heat them according to package instructions. If you’re making them from scratch, roll out the dough and cook on a griddle until golden brown and cooked through.
  • Shred the Roti: Let the cooked roti cool slightly, then cut or tear it into small strips or pieces.
2. Prepare the Chicken
  • Cook the Chicken: If you haven’t already cooked the chicken, you can either grill, bake, or sauté it until fully cooked. Season with salt and pepper. Once cooked, chop it into small pieces.
3. Prepare the Stir-fry
  • Heat Oil: In a large pan or wok, heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil over medium heat.
  • Sauté Aromatics: Add the finely chopped onion, garlic, and ginger to the pan. Sauté until the onions are translucent and fragrant.
  • Add Chilies and Vegetables Add green chilies, julienned carrot, and bell pepper (if using). Stir-fry for a few minutes until the vegetables start to soften.
  • Add Cabbage: Add the shredded cabbage and cook until it starts to wilt.
  • Add Chicken: Add the chopped cooked chicken to the pan. Stir well to mix with the vegetables.
4. Season and Combine
  • Add Seasonings: Stir in the soy sauce, curry powder, turmeric powder, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Cook for a few minutes until the spices are well combined and aromatic.
  • Add Roti: Add the shredded roti to the pan. Stir well to mix the roti with the vegetables and chicken. Cook for a few minutes, allowing the roti to absorb the flavors. The mixture should be a bit crunchy and not mushy.
  • Optional Eggs: Push the kottu mixture to one side of the pan. Pour the beaten eggs into the empty side and scramble them. Once cooked, mix the eggs into the kottu.
  • Tomato Sauce (Optional): If you like a slightly tangy flavor, add tomato sauce and mix well.
5.Serve
  • Adjust Seasoning: Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed.
  • Garnish: Garnish with freshly chopped cilantro
  • Serve: Serve hot with lemon wedges on the side for extra zest.

    • Enjoy your flavorful Chicken Kottu!





Cheese Kottu

Ingredients of Cheese Kottu

For the Kottu Base
  • 4-6 pieces of roti (paratha) or flatbread (store-bought or homemade)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
For the Stir-fry
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, mincedr
  • 1-inch piece ginger, minced
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 1 cup cabbage, shredded
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped (optional)
  • 1 cup cooked chicken, beef, or your choice of meat, chopped into small pieces (optional)
  • 2-3 eggs, beaten (optional for extra protein)
For Cheese the Seasoning
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, mozzarella, or a mix of your choice)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon tomato sauce (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

Instructions

1. Prepare the Roti
  • Cook the Roti: If you’re using store-bought roti, heat them according to package instructions. If you’re making them from scratch, roll out the dough and cook on a griddle until golden brown and cooked through.
  • Shred the Roti: Let the cooked roti cool slightly, then cut or tear it into small strips or pieces.
2. Prepare the Stir-fry
  • Heat Oil: In a large pan or wok, heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil over medium heat.
  • Sauté Aromatics: Add the finely chopped onion, garlic, and ginger to the pan. Sauté until the onions are translucent and fragrant.
  • Add Chilies and Vegetables Add green chilies, julienned carrot, and bell pepper (if using). Stir-fry for a few minutes until the vegetables start to soften.
  • Add Cabbage: Add the shredded cabbage and cook until it starts to wilt.
  • Add Meat (Optional): If using meat, add the cooked chicken, beef, or any other protein. Stir well to combine with the vegetables.
3. Add Cheese and Seasoning
  • Add Seasonings: Stir in the soy sauce, curry powder, turmeric powder, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Cook for a few minutes until the spices are well combined and aromatic.
  • Add Roti: Add the shredded roti to the pan. Stir well to mix the roti with the vegetables and chicken. Cook for a few minutes, allowing the roti to absorb the flavors. The mixture should be a bit crunchy and not mushy.
  • Add Cheese: Sprinkle the shredded cheese over the mixture. Stir well until the cheese melts and combines with the roti and vegetables. You can cover the pan with a lid for a minute or two to help the cheese melt more evenly.
  • Optional Eggs: Push the kottu mixture to one side of the pan. Pour the beaten eggs into the empty side and scramble them. Once cooked, mix the eggs into the kottu.
  • Tomato Sauce (Optional): If you like a slightly tangy flavor, add tomato sauce and mix well.
5. Serve
  • Adjust Seasoning: Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed.
  • Garnish: Garnish with freshly chopped cilantro
  • Serve: Serve hot with lemon wedges on the side for extra zest.

    • Enjoy your cheesy and flavorful Cheese Kottu!
